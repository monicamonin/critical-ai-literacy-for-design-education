# critical-ai-literacy-for-design-education

How can we develop critical AI literacy for design students in higher education?

This two-part workshop focuses on fostering students critical AI literacy through critical and reflective play with generative models. The workshops outlined below and accompanying worksheets were developed in the Visual Communication degree at the University of Technology Sydney (UTS) by Dr Andrew Burrell and Dr Monica Monin. The workshop and worksheets can be easily modified to meet the requirements for other disciplines.

# Workshop Structure

## Workshop 1 (~1hr)
In workshop 1, students working in groups of 4-6 collaboratively write their own set of 6 characteristics for a purposeful and responsible use of AI in design (or other discipline). 

# Workshop 2 (~1hr)
In workshop 2, students use generative image models to to visualise a design practice or generate images of a design outcome that embody any of the characteristics from the list they developed in Workshop 1. Students then undertake a visual analysis of the generated outcomes with questions considerate of how generative image models work.
